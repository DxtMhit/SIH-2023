Dropout Rates in Schools in India: An Analysis Drop-Out Ratio Data

Education is the foundation of a prosperous and empowered society. Education plays a vital role in the development of a nation. However, dropout rates in Indian schools have been a major cause of concern for policymakers and educators. It is also a significant issue in many countries worldwide, especially in developing countries.

The dropout rate is measured in terms of the percentage of students who leave school before completing their level/grade. It is a critical indicator of the effectiveness of the education system. The gov.in data reveals that the overall dropout rate in schools in India is 4.5 percent, which is lower than the previous year’s rate of 5.5 percent. However, the rate is still a concern, especially in certain states.

The gov.in data shows that the dropout rate is highest at the secondary level (9-10) with 17.5 percent, followed by upper primary (6-8) with 3.7 percent and primary (1-5) with 4.14 percent. The data further reveals that the dropout rate is higher for girls than boys at all levels of education.

The data also reveals that boys are likelier to drop out of school than girls in all three categories. The average dropout rate for girls in primary school is 3.88 percent, compared to 4.36 percent for boys. In upper primary, the dropout rate for 4.6 girls is percent, while it is 3.49percent for boys. Similarly, in secondary school, the dropout rate for girls is 16.88 percent, while it is 17.41percent for boys.

Let us take a look at the state-wise dropout rates:

 The state-wise data reveals some interesting trends. In some states, the dropout rate for girls is significantly higher than for boys. For instance, in Assam, the dropout rate for girls in upper primary school is 10.56percent, compared to 10.45 percent for boys. In secondary school, the rate is even higher, with girls dropping out at 29.28 percent, while it is 24.64percent for boys. Some states, however, have a lower dropout rate than the national average.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The dropout rate in Andaman and Nicobar Islands is relatively low, with 12.72 percent at the secondary level. However, there is a significant difference between boys and girls, with girls having a dropout rate of 11.47percent compared to boys at 8.9 percent.

Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh has a high dropout rate of 15.71 percent at the secondary level, much higher than the national average. The dropout rate is also higher for girls than boys at all levels of education.

Arunachal Pradesh

The dropout rate in Arunachal Pradesh is the highest in the country, with 17.11 percent at the secondary level. The dropout rate is also significantly high at the upper primary level at 6.71 percent. The rate is much higher for girls than boys at all levels of education.

Assam

Assam has a dropout rate of 27.06 percent at the secondary level, higher than the national average. The rate is also higher for girls than boys at all levels of education.

Bihar

Bihar has a dropout rate of 25.9 percent at the secondary level, which is also higher than the national average. The rate is much higher for girls than boys at all levels of education.

Chandigarh

Chandigarh has zero dropout rates at all levels of education. Chhattisgarh has a dropout rate of 21.26 percent at the secondary level, higher than the national average. The rate is also higher for girls than boys at all levels of education.

Dadra & Nagar Haveli & Daman and Diu

The dropout rate in Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu is relatively low, with 16.77percent at the secondary level. However, there is a significant difference between boys and girls, with girls having a dropout rate of 15.08 percent compared to boys at 18.19 percent.

Delhi

The dropout rate in Delhi is relatively low, with 11.81 percent at the secondary level. However, there is a significant difference between boys and girls, with girls having a dropout rate of 9.8 percent compared to boys at 13.55 percent.

Goa

The dropout rate in Goa is relatively low, with 11.15 percent at the secondary level. However, there is a significant difference between boys and girls, with girls having a dropout rate of 7.68 percent compared to boys at 14.15 percent.

Kerala

Kerala has a relatively low dropout rate compared to other states in India. The dropout rate in Kerala is also low as to null, with only around 0.0 percent of students dropping out of primary school and 12.32 percent dropping out of secondary school.

Meghalaya

The dropout rate for girls in primary school is 8.56percent, compared to 10.35 percent for boys.

Rajasthan

Rajasthan has a high dropout rate, particularly for girls. The dropout rate in Rajasthan is also high, with around 5.02 percent of students dropping out of primary school and 13.48 percent dropping out of secondary school.

Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu has a relatively low dropout rate compared to other states in India. The dropout rate in Tamil Nadu is as low as null, with 8.1 percent of students dropping out of secondary school.

Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh has a high dropout rate, particularly for girls in secondary. The dropout rate in Uttar Pradesh is also high, with around 8.58percent of students dropping out of primary school and 10.22 percent dropping out of secondary school.

The COVID-19 pandemic may have affected the dropout rates, and this data only covers the 2021-22 academic year. The report also indicates that some states have not provided complete data, and the dropout rates may be higher than reflected in the report.

High dropout rates are a cause for concern, as they significantly impact students’ prospects. Dropout rates are often linked to socio-economic factors, poverty & lack of access to quality education. Therefore, it is vital to address these underlying issues to reduce dropout rates and ensure that all students have the opportunity to complete their education
